Bamboozled!

A fun event where we test one’s thirst of knowledge in the fields of movies, literature, plays and so on. While doing so each team will also have to have to complete certain challenges like puzzles and crosswords.

Venue

New Audi/ Secondary Stage

Duration

2 hours for prelims and 2 hours for final round

Timing

Sunday 11:00am-1:00pm

Co-ordinators

Amruta Patil 8769809648 amrutapatil205@gmail.com

Nilesh Rakhecha 9920847389 nileshrakhecha23@gmail.com

Rules

Round 1

This round will be a crossword round in which every team will have to complete the crossword with a given time limit. The crossword will be around facts from the world of literature and pop culture. The teams who fail to complete the crossword in the given time limit will get eliminated immediately. Teams will get points based on how fast they complete the task the number of questions they get right.

Round 2

Following up with second round, participants will be asked to pick up a chit (one from each team) and the other members can ask maximum 20 questions to guess what's written on the chit, with the time limit of 5 mins. The member who picks up the chit can only answer the questions using yes or no.

Round 3

Only five teams will go through for the last round, these teams will be the ones who have the highest points. These five teams will compete on a life-sized snake and ladders board. Where instead of rolling a dice, each team will proceed based on the answers they give. Only on team member will be on the board while the rest will be seated.

Just as the rules of snakes and ladders if one reaches the bottom of a ladder then they must climb up the ladder. But if you get bitten by a snake you come down the snake. The twist is that before this round starts each team will be allotted different starting points on the board based on the number of points they have acquired over the last two rounds. The team with the highest points will be placed higher up on the board and the one with the least of the five teams will be placed at 0. Each tile on the board will have a multiple choice question which needs to be answered within 30seconds. If the team does give the correct answer then based on their time they will take a certain number of steps on the board. And if the team fails to answer the question then they remain on the same tile and in their next turn will receive a new question. The team to reach the last tile first will be the team that wins.

Steps taken according to time :
● 30s - 1step forward
● 20s - 3steps forward
● 10s - 5steps forward
● 5s- 7steps forward

Ekphrasis

Inspired from MoMA's collaborations with artists and writers, Litmus presents Exphrasis, a creative writing event for everyone who has a love for art and a way with words. Enliven your words and breath life into art as you practice ekphrasis : the skill of using art as a means to create new art.

Sunday 11:00 am-1:00 pm

Co-ordinators

Neha Seth : +919776843313 email: nehasheth1998@gmail.com

Rules


Round 1

Writing from a given prompt.
1. Each participant is given a story/poetry prompt. They are required to write a poem of 150-200 words or a story of 250-300 words in 25 minutes.
2. Grading is done on a scale of 0 to 10.

Round 2

Writing based on a given artwork.
1. Each participant is given an abstract artwork or a visual image. They are required to write a story or poem based on it in a time duration of 30 minutes.
2. Grading is done on a scale of 0-10.
3. Final score is calculated out of 20, and 3 winners are selected.
4. Top 5 entries get their writeups published in the college magazine.

General Rules

1. Use of mobile phones or access of the internet by any means is prohibited.
2. In case of tie breakers, additional prompts will be given and the team with the higher score will be qualified for the next round.
3. CCs and OCs of the event have the authority to make any changes with the event details and the venue.
4. Decision of the panel of judges is final.

Just A Minute

Just A Minute

Just a Minute (JAM) has evolved from a BBC radio show with the same name. It’s a fun event comprising of public speaking and spontaneous thinking. Participants not only get to show off their public speaking skills but also their creativity.

Venue

Secondary Stage

Timing

Saturday 1:00pm-3:00pm

Co-ordinators

Paridhi Agrawal: 9930006670
Nilesh Rakhecha : 9920847389

Rules

Round 1

1. Each participant will have to pick up a chit before the event starts. This chit will be your participant ID.
2. There will be a panel of five at a time. This panel of five will be made based on the participant I’d number.
3. The challenge for each speaker is to speak for a continuous minute without making any errors of any sorts. These errors can be stammering, grammatical errors, use of the wrong language and deviation from the topic.
4. Each member of the panel picks up a chit just before their turn, this chit will contain the speakers topic.
5. If the speaker does make an error, then the other members of the panel can JAM the speaker. On his/her appeal for a JAM the anchor would ask on what grounds does this JAM stand valid. If the reason given is correct then the participant gets to continue to talk for the remaining 1 minute on the same topic.
6. Each participant will be given 1 point for every time they JAM another participant. Loss of a point if the JAM stands invalid. Also the participant who ends at the 1 minute mark will receive 3 points.

Round 2

Participants who score the highest in their respective panels will go through this round.
1. Each participant will be asked a set of 8 abstract questions.
2. The participant is expected to NOT answer in respect to the question.
3. If the participant does answer in respect to the question then the round for that participant is over there itself.
4. Participants will be marked with 1 point for each question they answer.

General Rules

All the rules of the first round are applicable. This is a theme based round. Each participant is given the same topic and each of them is given a different theme (ie emotions). The participants have to speak on the given topic in relation to the theme for one minute The winner of the event will be declared on the grounds of the total points accumulated at the end of three rounds.

British Parliamentary Debate

Parliamentary Debate

British Parliamentary Debate is a form of debate wherein four teams of two compete on a topic that is given on the spot with a preparation time of 15 minutes. The floor is divided amongst the Government and the Opposition. The four teams, randomly allotted at the time of debate, are the Opening Government (Prime Minister, Deputy Prime Minister), Opening Opposition (Leader of Opposition, Deputy Leader of Opposition), Closing Government (Member of Government, Government Whip) and Closing Opposition (Member of Opposition, Opposition Whip). The Government and Opposition compete explicitly against each other, while all four teams compete implicitly as well. The functions of the teams and their members individually will be distributed in the event invite itself.
